Work and energy¶

The Newton-Euler equations relate forces to acceleration. Forces and accelrations are vector quantities. Work and energy are scalar quantities that result from

  • \(\mathbf{F} \cdot d \mathbf{r} = dW\)

  • \(m \mathbf{a} \cdot d \mathbf{r} = dT\)

where \(d \mathbf{r}\) is the change position of an object and \(dW\) is the work done to the object and \(dT\) is the change in kinetic energy of object.

Conservation of energy¶

The first law of thermodynamics

\[T_1 + V_1 + W^{1\rightarrow 2} = T_2 + V_2\]

where

  • \(T_1,~T_2\): kinetic energy

  • \(V_1,~V_2\): potential energy

  • $W^{1\rightarrow 2}: work done to the system (not included in the potential energy changes)
